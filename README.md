## Reason
I believe that the publisher **Secret Mode** would be a great option for my video game. This is mainly because, from the video games I have seen them publish, they seem to focus more into publishing 3D video games with a cartoonish look, which follows the aesthetic of my game. They also seem to focus on publishing video games made by indie developers, as well as having multiple entries with the **Action** tag. All of this follows the same types my video game has and tries to portray.

They seem to have an average of **86%** in **Review Scores**, having a maximum of **98%** (in **Wobbledogs**, **Loddlenaut**) and a minimum of **63%** (in **Parcel Corps**). They also seem to have quite a decent amount of sales on their products, with its smallest number of copies sold being **1.6k** (in **Pine Hearts**), and its highest number of copies sold being **6.7m** (in **Escape the Backrooms**), with **Pine Hearts** costing around **$8.5 USD** (**~$149 MXN**) and **Escape the Backrooms** costing around **$6.5 USD** (**~$115 MXN**).

With this in mind, I could sell my video game in a price range of **$5 USD** (**~$90 MXN**) at the minimum and **$7 USD** (**~$125 MXN**) at the maximum. While individually it does not amount to much, if the median between the **smallest copies sold** (**1.6k**) and the **highest copies sold** (**6.7m**) is taken into consideration, then I would be gaining **~3,350,800** (this is only the median). Realistically speaking, while highly unlikely to happen, even at the lowest copies sold, I would be gaining **~$8,000 USD - ~$11,200 USD** (using the current conversion between USD to MXN: 17.78, then the range would be: **~$142,241.36 MXN - ~$199,137.90 MXN**) in total.
